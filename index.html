The Alayon and Kapinangan Hall: Named after the two known wives of Datu Sumakwel, the chief of Panay and the ruler of Hantik (now Antique), the walls of these halls lie the adventurous and fascinating story of Alayon, the enduring love and Kapinangan, the unfaithful wife, who turns out to be one and the same.

Like Alayon and Kapinangan, the two function halls are jointly similar to the other, like the love and affection of Datu Sumakwel to both but are distinctly different in terms of character and impact just like their roles in the life of the Datu.

ALAYON HALL

Bigger and more spacious. Like Alayon, holds greater and deeper secrets about her true self.

KAPINANGAN HALL

More intimate space. Like Kapinangan contains her unlikely unpleasant past restricted.
